Engineering & Robotics Projects

Explore the designs, construction processes, and technologies used in my hands-on creations.

Bluetooth-Controlled Lego EV3 Car

This project involved creating a remote-controlled car using the official Lego Mindstorms EV3 kit. The primary challenge was programming the EV3 Intelligent Brick to establish and maintain a stable Bluetooth connection with an external custom controller (often a smartphone or tablet interface).

  • Technology: Lego Mindstorms EV3, Bluetooth Communication, EV3-G graphical programming language (or Python/Scratch equivalent).
  • Construction: Careful assembly ensuring the structural integrity to withstand quick starts and stops, focusing on gear ratios for optimal speed vs. torque.
  • Skills Demonstrated: Robotics control, wireless communication protocol implementation, debugging hardware/software interaction.

Upcycled RC Glider Project

This build focused heavily on applying aeronautical principles and sustainability. I salvaged the motor, battery, and remote electronics from a discarded RC car. The new airframe was meticulously crafted from lightweight cardboard, requiring multiple iterations to perfect the airfoil shape and ensure the center of gravity was correctly balanced for stable flight.

  • Key Focus: Aerodynamics, weight distribution, material science (lightweight cardboard construction).
  • Process: Measured, cut, and assembled custom wings and stabilizers. Required careful soldering and wiring of the salvaged components.
  • Outcome: A functional, lightweight glider capable of being remotely steered after a successful launch.

Arduino Steering Wheel (Unojoy)

I utilized an Arduino Uno microcontroller and the Unojoy framework to create a custom steering wheel that could interface with PC games. This project deepened my understanding of how analog inputs (like a rotary sensor or potentiometer) are read by a microcontroller, converted into digital data, and then sent to a computer, simulating a standard game controller.

  • Technology: Arduino Uno, Unojoy library, Potentiometer (for steering input), custom button inputs.
  • Coding: Implementing C++ code in the Arduino IDE to map physical rotation to game controller axis data.
  • Learning: Gained experience in microcontrollers, input/output (I/O) hardware interfacing, and device drivers.
